What is OPT?

Optional Practical Training (OPT) allows F-1 students to work in the US for up to 12 months after graduation, with possible 24-month STEM extension.

‚è≥

Timing is Critical

File between 90 days before and 60 days after graduation. OPT must start within 60 days of graduation.

üìä

Required Documents

I-765 form, passport photos, I-20 with OPT recommendation, transcripts, and payment.

üíé

Filing Fee

Current filing fee is $410. Additional biometrics fee may apply.

Preparation Phase

Essential steps to take before filing your OPT application

1

Meet with Your DSO

Schedule an appointment with your Designated School Official (DSO) to discuss OPT eligibility and requirements.

What to bring:
  • Current passport
  • I-94 record
  • Academic transcripts
  • Graduation date confirmation
2

Request OPT Recommendation

Your DSO will update your SEVIS record and provide a new I-20 with OPT recommendation.

Timeline: Request at least 2-3 weeks before you plan to file
3

Gather Required Documents

Collect all necessary documents for your application package.

Required documents:
  • Form I-765 (Application for Employment Authorization)
  • Passport-style photos (2 copies)
  • Copy of current I-20 with OPT recommendation
  • Copy of passport biographical page
  • Copy of I-94 arrival record
  • Copy of F-1 visa
  • Copy of transcripts or diploma
  • Payment method (check, money order, or credit card)

Completing Form I-765

Step-by-step instructions for filling out the employment authorization application

Part 1: Personal Information

Enter your family name exactly as it appears on your passport and I-20.

Enter your given name exactly as it appears on your passport and I-20.

Enter your middle name if applicable, or leave blank if none.

List any other names you have used, including maiden name if applicable.

Part 2: Information About Your Eligibility

Select "(c)(3)(A)" for pre-completion OPT or "(c)(3)(B)" for post-completion OPT.

Write: "F-1 student seeking post-completion optional practical training."

Part 3: Additional Information

If you have any additional information that supports your application, include it here.

Important Notes

  • Use black ink and print clearly
  • Do not leave any fields blank - use "N/A" if not applicable
  • Sign and date the form in blue ink
  • Make a copy of the completed form for your records

Tracking Your Application

How to monitor your OPT application status and what to expect

Receipt Notice

You should receive a receipt notice within 2-3 weeks of filing. This contains your receipt number for tracking.

Biometrics Appointment

If required, you'll receive a biometrics appointment notice. Attend this appointment to provide fingerprints and photo.

Processing Times

Current processing times vary by location but typically range from 3-5 months. Check USCIS processing times for updates.

Approval Notice

Upon approval, you'll receive Form I-766 (Employment Authorization Document) and approval notice.
